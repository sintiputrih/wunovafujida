Trisha Krishnan Wiki/Biography

Born on 04th May 1983, Trisha Krishnan’s age is 38 Years as of 2021. She was born in Chennai which was known as Madras back then.

Trisha Krishnan Childhood Photo

She completed her initial education at the Sacred Heart Matriculation Hr. Sec School, Church Park, Chennai, and her higher education at the Ethiraj College for women in Chennai. She completed her bachelor’s in business administration from Ethiraj college. While in college, she worked in various television and print commercials.

Trisha wanted to become a criminal psychologist but then she gave up the idea to pursue acting. In 1999 Trisha won the ‘Miss Salem’ beauty contest and later she won the Miss Chennai contest. In 2000, she appeared in Falguni Pathak’s music video ‘Meri Chunnar Ud Ud Jae’. She played the role of Ayesha Takia’s friend in that song. Trisha started doing assignments while she was in college and she had to work hard to accommodate and make proper time for her education.

Career

Trisha’s first theatrical release was Mounam Pesiyadhe in 2002. This movie was directed by Ameer and it also starred Surya Sivakumar.  Although this was her first theatrical release, the first project she accepted was Priyadarshan directed by Lesa Lesa. This film featured Sham and Trisha in the lead roles. In 2003, she got an offer to feature in A.R. Rahman’s musical Enakku 20 Unakku 18. These movies did not do very well at the box office.

In 2003, she played a cancer patient in a Tamil movie titled Manasellam. This movie was directed by Santosh. After that, she appeared in K. Balachander’s Saamy. This movie was an instant with the audiences and critics alike and Trisha started getting a lot of movie offers after that. In 2003, she appeared in another romantic movie called ‘Alai’ which was directed by Vikram Kumar. This movie bombed at the box office.

In 2004, she made her debut in Telugu cinema with ‘Varsham’. This movie was directed by MS Raju and had Prabhas and Trisha in the lead. This movie was a blockbuster hit. She was offered to reprise the same role in the Tamil remake titled ‘Mazhai’ but she declined the offer. In the same year, she was paired opposite Vijay in an action-comedy ‘Ghili’ which was a huge commercial success. This movie was directed by Dharani. After that, she appeared in ‘Ayutha Ezuthu’ which was a Mani Ratnam movie but it failed to do very well at the box office.

In the next few years, Trisha had 12 releases and she played the female lead in 11 of them. Her second Telugu project was Prabhu Deva’s directorial debut Nuvvostanante Nenoddantana in which she was paired opposite Siddhartha. Trisha earned her first Filmfare award and Nandi award for the same. Her subsequent releases ‘Athi’ and ‘Ji’ both bombed at the box office.
