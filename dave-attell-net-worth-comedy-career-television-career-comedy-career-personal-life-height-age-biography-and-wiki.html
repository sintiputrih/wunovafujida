Stand-Up Comedy Career

Dave’s debut in stand-up comedy took place at Governor’s in Levittown, which he described as a “total bomb.”

His first TV appearance was on VH1’s “Stand-Up Spotlight” in 1988, followed by his debut on “The Late Show with David Letterman” in 1993.

Lorne Michaels, impressed by Dave’s performance, recruited him as a writer for “Saturday Night Live,” where he contributed to 20 episodes and appeared in seven.

Subsequently, Attell was featured in the “HBO Comedy Showcase” (1995) and the “1995 Young Comedians Special.”

In 1996, he had his own “HBO Comedy Half-Hour” special. His comedy journey included appearances in “Pulp Comics” (1997), “Mr. Vegas All-Night Party Starring Drew Carey” (1997), “Just for Laughs” (1997), and “The N.Y. Friars Club Roast of Drew Carey” (1998).

In 2001, he received an American Comedy Award nomination for Funniest Male Stand-Up Comic and released his first comedy album, “Skanks for the Memories…” in 2003.

In 2006, Attell released the DVD “Hey, Your Mouth’s Not Pregnant!” and, the next year, HBO aired “Captain Miserable,” his first one-hour special. His one-hour special “Road Work” aired on Comedy Central in 2014.

Dave toured with Jeff Ross in 2018, and they created a three-part Netflix documentary titled “Bumping Mics with Jeff Ross & Dave Attell.
