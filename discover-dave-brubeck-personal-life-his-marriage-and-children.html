Discover Dave Brubeck Personal Life: His Marriage and Children

September 2024 · 2 minute read

Dave Brubeck was an American jazz pianist and composer who died in 2012 with a net worth of $15 million. Dave Brubeck is regarded as one of the forefathers of the cool jazz genre. He was well-known for his use of strange time signatures and opposing rhythms in his music, which can be heard on his most popular album, “Time Out.”

Throughout the 1950s and 1960s, he recorded numerous records with the Dave Brubeck Quartet and traversed the world on behalf of the United States Department of State, performing jazz music in Europe and Asia. Among his many honors are the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award and his appearance on the cover of “Time” magazine in 1954.

Who was Dave Brubeck married to?

In September 1942, Dave Brubeck married jazz lyricist Iola Whitlock; the couple was married for 70 years until his death in 2012. Iola died of cancer on March 12, 2014, at the age of 90, in Wilton, Connecticut.

In 2000, Brubeck and his wife, Iola, established the Brubeck Institute at their alma school, the University of the Pacific. What began as a special archive containing the Brubecks’ personal document collection has since expanded to provide fellowships and educational opportunities in jazz for students, as well as having one of the main streets on which the school is located named Dave Brubeck Way in his honor.[34]

Brubeck joined the Jazz Foundation of America in its quest to safeguard the homes and lives of senior jazz and blues performers, especially those who survived Hurricane Katrina, in 2008.[35] Brubeck participated in the Jazz Foundation’s annual benefit event “A Great Night in Harlem” in order to support the organization.

How many children did Dave Brubeck own?

Brubeck and Iola have six children. His four children are all professional musicians. Darius, the eldest and named after Brubeck’s instructor Darius Milhaud, is a pianist, producer, educator, and performer. Dan is a percussionist, Chris is a multi-instrumentalist and composer, and Matthew is a cello with considerable composition and performance credits. Michael, another son, died in 2009. Brubeck’s children frequently performed with him in concerts and in the recording studio.
