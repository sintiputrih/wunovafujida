Jeff Sims- Professional Career

Jeff started his career in high school, where he was the team’s starting quarterback his sophomore year. He was interested in playing for the Miami Hurricanes after high school, but they were not interested in him. As a result, he shifted his focus to the Florida State Seminoles. He committed to Florida in 2019 as a backup quarterback to James Blackman. He was spotted taking part in the Elite 11 quarterback competition. He departed Florida in December 2019 when the coach was replaced. He inked a contract with the Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets on December 18, 2019.

The Atlanta Journal-Constitution reported that he would be the team’s starting quarterback. In his collegiate debut, he scored a 16-13 triumph over the Florida State Seminoles at Doak Campbell Stadium. He has completed 24 of 35 throws for 277 yards and has gained 64 yards on the ground. He was named to the Davey O’ Brien Award and Manning Award weekly lists. In addition, he was defeated by the UCF Knights by a score of 49-21 and by the Syracuse Orange by a score of 37-20. In addition, he threw four interceptions.
