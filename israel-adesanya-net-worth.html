Israel Adesanya’s Early Career and UFC Journey

Israel Adesanya’s journey in combat sports started with kickboxing, where he showcased his incredible talent and dedication. As an amateur kickboxer, he boasted an unbeaten record of 32-0, establishing himself as a force to be reckoned with in the ring.

Transitioning to the professional scene, Adesanya continued to make waves in kickboxing, competing under the promotional banners of King in The Ring and GLORY. Throughout his career, Adesanya engaged in a fierce rivalry with fellow kickboxer Alex Pereira.

However, Adesanya’s aspirations extended beyond kickboxing, leading him to pursue a career in mixed martial arts (MMA). In 2017, he made his debut in the Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC), the premier MMA organization in the world.

Since joining the UFC, Adesanya has achieved remarkable success. He embarked on a phenomenal winning streak, securing victories in nine consecutive fights and capturing the Middleweight championship.

Adesanya’s journey in the UFC has seen him face some of the most formidable opponents in the division, including legendary fighters like Anderson Silva, Kelvin Gastelum, Robert Whittaker, and Jan Blachowicz.

This multi-disciplinary success in kickboxing and MMA has solidified Adesanya’s position as one of the top fighters in the UFC, earning him a reputation as a skilled and versatile athlete.
