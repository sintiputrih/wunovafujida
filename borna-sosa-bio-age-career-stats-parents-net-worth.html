Borna Sosa Bio

Born Sosa was born in the Prečko neighbourhood of Zagreb to Croats from Gradac, near Posušje. He is a versatile left back defender and a wing back who plays for the Croatia national team and for Bundesliga club VfB Stuttgart.

Borna Sosa is a product of the Dinamo Zagreb youth team system. He started playing for t he youth side of Dinamo Zagreb in 2005. After playing for ten years, he was promoted to the senior team of Dinamo Zagreb in 2015. He played three years and was moved to the team B side of Dinamo Zagreb.

On 14th May 2018, Borna Sosa signed a five-year-contract with the Bundesliga club, VfB Stuttgart, in Germany.

Sosa represented Croatia at the 2015 UEFA Under-17 Euro and the 2015 FIFA U-17 World Cup. In May 2018, he was named in Zlatko Dalić’s preliminary 32-man squad for the 2018 World Cup in Russia, but did not make the final 23. He was named in Nenad Gračan’s squad for the UEFA Under-21 Euro 2019 and Igor Bišćan’s squad for the UEFA Under-21 Euro 2021. However, he was ruled out of the latter squad at the last minute due to a knee injury.

Under Germany coach Joachim Löw’s initiative, Sosa was given German citizenship on 7th May 2021, through his mother Vesna who was born and raised in Berlin, and was expected to represent Germany at the UEFA Euro 2020. A day later, on 8th May, Sosa himself confirmed the information. However, on 11th May, Oliver Bierhoff, the technical director of the German Football Association, announced that Sosa is ineligible to play for Germany according to the FIFA rules. Sosa issued an official apology to the Croatian public, Croatia national team fans and the Croatian Football Federation via the Federation’s website, two days later, on 13th May, 2021.

Sosa earned his first call-up to the national team on 16th August 2021, ahead of the September World Cup qualifiers against Russia, Slovakia and Slovenia. Sosa is part of the 26-man-squad of the Croatian National Team at the 2022 Qatar FIFA World Cup.
