Kim Baldonado NBC4

Baldonado is a reporter for NBC4 LA. She perfected her writing, reporting, and editing skills which enabled her to get her first job as an air reporter in Palm Springs with KESQ before joining NBC4.

She started her broadcast journalism career in San Francisco. Baldonado served as an assignment editor prior to returning to Los Angeles to become a field producer for KTTV; throughout this period, she sharpened her writing, reporting, and editing skills

Baldonado is fairly comfortable broadcasting on breaking news and can be found on feature pieces, that she writes and produces. She is known for addressing complex matters in her news stories. Her well-known storytelling and writing are often presented in a thought-provoking way that enlightens and educates.

Baldonado has been awarded for changing the face of homelessness in Southern California and taking on such topics as the state of public education in Los Angeles. Additionally, she is impassioned about reporting stories that are historically important, like for instance her coverage of Pope John Paul II’s important visit to Cuba, which led to progress in religious freedom for the Communist nation. She as well covered the massive Latino and immigrant civil rights march in Washington, D.C., and the Summer Olympics in Sydney, Australia.

Being a Southern California native, She was raised in San Gabriel and is known for her community work. She offers countless hours talking to pupils about the significance of taking a college degree. Baldonado works on the Board of Directors for Big Brothers Big Sisters of Los Angeles, in which she is also a volunteer mentor and was identified by the group with the Excellence in Mentoring Award.

Kim Baldonado Awards

Through her tenure at NBC4, she has been honored with numerous professional awards. She was able to win Los Angeles-area Emmy Awards for live news coverage, social issues, and education, and a prestigious National Edward R. Murrow Award for her work on the half-hour special “LAUSD: Battle for Control,”. Her extra honor compromise of 5 Golden Mikes and an Associated Press Mark Twain Award.
